## 0. The "so what" (30 seconds)

synvert xgeeks organises KCD Porto. That role has built warm relationships with **60+ cloud-native
companies** and **~€59K of closed 2025 sponsorships** (~€33K in 2024), with **~20 returning
sponsors** — plus **bulk-ticket-buyer contacts** (e.g. Siemens, NOS) and a **full 2024–2025 speaker
database**. The same companies are exactly the product and enterprise engineering orgs that buy
nearshore cloud, platform, data and AI engineering — i.e. xgeeks' core business.

**The recommendation:** run a deliberate, **dual-track** motion that opens commercial conversations
across **many channels — before, during and after KCD** — using the existing relationships as warm
doors, **without ever compromising the sponsorship or KCD's vendor-neutral integrity.** The dinners
matter, but they are **one door among many**, not the whole plan. Measure on **€ pipeline influenced
over 6–12 months at a ≥4:1 pipeline-to-cost ratio** — not contact counts.

Plain-language note: **ABM** = pick a short list of specific high-value companies and treat each as
its own mini-campaign. **Pipeline influenced, not lead volume** = measure the € of real conversations
and deals opened, not vanity counts. **Slot into the gaps** = time business touches for moments that
don't clash with closing the sponsorship or running the event.

## 4. The dual-track rule + the hand-off

1. **Sequencing / do no harm.** Sponsorship first; open business only after it's secure or clearly
   on track, separated in time and through a different person. Never open BD mid-negotiation.
2. **Never pay-to-play, either direction.** Sponsoring ≠ business; business ≠ sponsorship perks.
3. **Separate voices.** Fábio stays the organizer; a different voice opens business.

**Who opens it — three hand-off patterns:**
- **A · Warm intro, then step back** — Fábio makes a light peer intro, then exits; no sponsorship
  mentioned, time-separated. Use for closed, strong, returning accounts.
- **B · Direct peer outreach (recommended default)** — Ivan / a sales-eng lead or manager reaches
  the route-in/target directly; Fábio never appears. Use for most accounts, always for
  in-negotiation ones.
- **C · On-the-floor** — a manager (Luís Soares, André, Ivan, Gui) engages as a peer on-site.
  Opportunistic.

**Green-light:** BD opens only on CLOSED / on-track accounts. Green today: MC Sonae, Critical
TechWorks. In-negotiation stays Fábio-only until signed.

## 5. Channel playbook — the doors (KCD is one door, not the only one)

### Before the event — remote & ahead-of-time (biggest window)
- **Email & LinkedIn** (most important) — peer message from the business voice, KCD hook, light
  transition to "could we help with X". Pre-warming lifts on-site meetings.
- **The existing logistics relationship** — via KCD we already talk to sponsors constantly
  (onboarding, T&C, invoices, booth, logistics). Warm ongoing line; once a sponsorship is closed the
  business track can open off it — through the separate voice, never inside a logistics email.
- **Bulk-ticket buyers** (Siemens, NOS, …) — already contacted; Fábio hands each to Ivan for a
  KCD-hook → exploratory-call sequence.
- **Past speakers (2024–25 DB)** — many at top cloud/AI firms; reach out ahead, framed on their work
  + community, business only where fit. Also feeds 2026 CFP & meetup speakers.

### During the event — on-site, peer-to-peer
- **Booth (first-class channel)** — staff our booth with 1–2 sales + engineering managers who walk
  to sponsor booths, ask about their stack and challenges, and host the same conversations at ours.
  Curiosity first, no pitch.
- **Speaker dinner** (Day 1 or Day 2 of KCD) + **guest dinner** (returning sponsors we're renewing +
  accounts in negotiation, e.g. Kuehne+Nagel; aim for senior signatories or someone who can reach
  them). Managers — Luís Soares, André, Ivan, Gui — simply present and work the room naturally. **No
  assigned seating, no scripts.** Capture lightly each day.

### Year-round & after
- **National meetups** (Braga → Lisbon) — low-pressure, speaker-format; identify sponsors who can
  provide speakers or host. Keeps relationships warm between editions.
- **Fast personal follow-up** — hot contacts within 24–48h; nurture toward deals over 6–18 months.

## 6. How to introduce the business topic (concrete, copy-pasteable)

- **Before — email/LinkedIn:** *"Hi [name] — great to have [company] at KCD Porto again. Outside the
  event hat: I'm on the engineering side at xgeeks and we do a lot of nearshore platform/cloud work.
  Saw you're scaling [X] — would a 20-min swap of notes before November be useful? No agenda, just
  peers."*
- **Before — bulk-ticket buyer (via Ivan):** *"Glad your team's joining KCD Porto. Since a few of you
  are coming, I thought I'd reach out from the xgeeks engineering side — we help teams like yours
  scale delivery. Worth a quick exploratory call?"*
- **Before — past speaker:** *"Loved your KCD talk on [topic]. We work on exactly that nearshore —
  would love to compare notes, and talk about how we help teams in [their space] if useful."*
- **During — booth (ours/theirs):** *"What are you building these days? … We run a lot of that
  nearshore — how's the platform side holding up as you scale?"* Curiosity first.
- **During — dinner (manager):** *"Loved your team's talk. We pick up exactly this kind of work —
  happy to connect you with one of our platform leads if it's ever useful, totally exploratory."*

**Never:** imply sponsoring buys business (or vice-versa); sell from the stage; pitch a competitor.
Off-stage, peer-to-peer, vendor-neutral — always. Respect the CNCF Code of Conduct.
