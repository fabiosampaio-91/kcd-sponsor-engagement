1.The thesis: we own the platform

Most companies pay to appear at someone else's conference — a booth, a slot, a logo on a banner. They compete for visibility on a stage they don't control. synvert xgeeks does the opposite. We created KCD Porto, we run it, and we shape it. We own the audience, the content, and the entire experience.

That distinction is the whole argument. When you own the platform, visibility is no longer something you buy at a premium and hope converts — it is something you manufacture, target, and compound. The event stops being a marketing expense and becomes a strategic asset.

The bottom line. KCD Porto turns community into credibility, and credibility into business impact — a category-ownership position at the national level that no competitor in Iberia holds.

It works for the business in four distinct ways, each covered below: a positioning engine, a business-development channel, a talent magnet, and a brand amplifier.

2.How we built it — a deliberate six-year bet

The asset was not an accident. It was a sequenced bet on expertise, community and content, born from a concrete commercial problem.

2.1 The positioning problem

synvert xgeeks was primarily positioned around engineering capacity. But clients stopped buying capacity alone — they wanted real technical expertise, especially in Cloud Native enablement. The question became: how do we reposition from "a software house" to a recognised technical authority?

2.2 The answer was community

We understood something fundamental: you don't build authority in tech without a community, and you don't attract top leads without being visible in that community. So we built through content — meetups (Cloud Native Lisboa and Porto) and deep, engineering-led talks. Credibility in action, not in claims.

2.3 Validation, then trust

  • CNCF certification (2023) — external validation via the Cloud Native Computing Foundation. We started winning projects with large enterprises.
  • CNCF trust — consistency led to trust, and CNCF trusted us to organise Kubernetes Community Days (KCD) in Portugal: a global, internationally recognised format. A strong signal that we were now a credible actor in the ecosystem.
From zero to #1 in two years. In 2024 we started from scratch — no legacy, no existing audience. By 2025, KCD Porto had become Iberia's largest Cloud Native event. Three things made it: community, expertise, and flawless execution.

4.The CNCF moat

The defensibility comes from a structural scarcity that competitors cannot simply spend their way past.

4.1 CNCF Silver Member

synvert xgeeks holds CNCF Silver Membership — one of the few Portuguese companies with this status. It is not a badge; it is an investment that unlocks influence, visibility and trust in the global cloud native community. With 98% of organisations adopting cloud native techniques and 82% running Kubernetes in production, being embedded in CNCF puts us at the centre of where the industry is heading.

4.2 Only KCD in Iberia

We organise the only two official CNCF meetups in Portugal — Cloud Native Lisbon and Cloud Native Porto. CNCF is increasingly selective about host cities, and Portugal was chosen again on track record, community feedback and execution quality. KCD Porto is the only KCD in Portugal and the Iberian Peninsula, at CNCF Tier 2 — the highest category allowed (max 500 pax), deliberately positioned not to compete with KubeCon.

Why it's a moat, not just a badge. It took six years to build. Competition for host status is fierce, and we were chosen again. Credibility of this kind cannot be shortcut — which is exactly why it is a durable competitive position.

5.The 2026 merger with DevOps Days Portugal

DevOps Days is a global conference series founded in Ghent in 2009 — 200+ editions across 40+ countries, a reference in the DevOps world. DevOps Days Portugal had run events in Porto before, and they approached us to join forces, recognising the scale and quality we had built.

The result: synvert xgeeks is now the legal entity behind two of the most recognised tech conference brands in Portugal, both with international reach. That is not just brand visibility — it is institutional weight.

8.Where it goes next

The long-term vision innovates within the format rather than abandoning it.

  • Continue at Tier 2 in Porto — the maximum CNCF allows, designed not to compete with KubeCon. Innovate within it: stronger international keynotes, thought-leadership tracks, community tracks, more gamification.
  • International keynotes — attract more global names from the CNCF ecosystem, elevating KCD Porto from a national event to a European reference for cloud native practitioners.
  • Thought-leadership track — a dedicated track for CTOs, VPs of Engineering and decision-makers, more closely aligned with business strategy than technical depth alone.
  • Community track & gamification — deeper engagement and retention across the whole year.
  • Annual anchor event — solidify KCD Porto as the go-to annual Cloud Native + DevOps event in Portugal.
The long bet: beyond KCD. The KCD and CNCF brands have limits. synvert xgeeks could create a Cloud Native Day Europe — or partner with existing KCDs across Europe — to build a European-scale event with synvert xgeeks at the centre.
