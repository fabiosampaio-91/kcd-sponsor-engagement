004 · The dual-track ruleThe hand-off
THE HAND-OFF WHO OPENS THE BUSINESS CONVERSATION
synvert xgeeks005 / 016

It does not have to be Fábio — and usually shouldn't be

Three ways to pass the baton.

Pattern A · Warm intro, then step back
Fábio connects, then exits
Fábio sends a light peer intro — "you should meet my colleague Ivan, he leads our platform work" — then steps out of the thread. No sponsorship mentioned; time-separated from any renewal ask so it never feels quid-pro-quo.
Use when: sponsorship already closed & the relationship is strong.
Pattern B · Direct peer outreach
Ivan / manager reaches out cold-warm
An xgeeks sales-eng lead or manager messages the route-in or target directly, referencing KCD & the community — Fábio never appears. Keeps the two roles fully separate.
Recommended default — especially for accounts still in sponsorship negotiation.
Pattern C · On-the-floor
A manager engages in person
At our booth or the dinner, a manager (Luís Soares, André, Ivan, Gui) talks shop as a peer; business emerges naturally from the conversation. Fábio is the host, not the salesperson.
Use when: opportunistic, on-site, signal already sensed.

Route-in = the warm person who opens the door. Target = the person who signs / holds budget. The hand-off is always: organizer relationship → separate business voice → the target, on neutral ground.

007 · SegmentationTiers + target/route-in
TIERS TARGET & ROUTE-IN
synvert xgeeks008 / 016

A tier = how much hands-on effort an account earns

Few accounts, treated deeply.

This is ABM — account-based marketing: instead of chasing many leads, we pick a short list of specific high-value companies and treat each as its own mini-campaign. For each we name the TARGET (signs the contract / holds budget — often no direct line) and the ROUTE-IN (the warm path to them — the Excel contact and any other relationships we've built inside that org).

Tier 11:1 · deepest
8 client accounts. Top fit, budget-holder identified, warm/returning. Named owner, dinner seat, pre-booked conversation, personal 48h follow-up. Sky, Critical Manufacturing, Blip, Feedzai, MC Sonae, Critical TechWorks, La Redoute, Euronext.
Tier 21:few
~19 accounts. Good fit. Segment outreach, dinner/roundtable spillover, prioritised booth conversations. e.g. VW DS, Farfetch, JYSK, Revolut, NOS, Bosch, Natixis, Coverflex, Kuehne+Nagel.
Tier 31:many
Remainder. Light-touch nurture; partner-referral track for vendors; market-intel only for competitors. Watch for upgrade signals.

Why route-in matters: we rarely have a direct line to the signatory. Example — at MC Sonae the warm route-in is the marketing/events contact, but we also know Luís Gabriel Monteiro, Head of Architecture & Platforms, a far better path to a real engineering conversation. We map and use every contact we hold inside each org, not just the booking contact.

011 · Before the eventDuring the event
DURING THE EVENT BOOTH & DINNERS
synvert xgeeks012 / 016

On-site — peer-to-peer, curiosity-first, no pitch

Work the floor, then the table.

The booth — a first-class channel
Staff our booth with 1–2 sales + engineering managers. Sponsors and their engineers are on-site all day — heads and managers are often physically there. Our people walk over to sponsor booths, ask about their stack and what they're building, and host the same curious conversations at ours. Get to know their engineers; understand the challenges. No pitch — peer interest first.
The dinners — one asset, not the plan
Speaker dinner (Day 1 or Day 2 of KCD) — peers, speakers, partners. Guest dinner — invite the returning 2024/2025 sponsors we're renewing and accounts in negotiation (e.g. Kuehne+Nagel), aiming for the senior signatories or someone who can reach them. Our managers — Luís Soares, André, Ivan, Gui and others — simply present and work the room naturally. No assigned seating, no scripts.

Capture, lightly: after each day, jot who we spoke to, their challenge, and the next step, so nothing is lost before follow-up. The point of being on-site is conversations, not badge scans.

012 · During the eventThe language
THE LANGUAGE COPY-PASTEABLE WORDING
synvert xgeeks013 / 016

Real messages for each setting — remote first

How to introduce the business topic.

Before · email / LinkedIn (most important)
"Hi [name] — great to have [company] at KCD Porto again. Outside the event hat: I'm on the engineering side at xgeeks and we do a lot of nearshore platform/cloud work. Saw you're scaling [X] — would a 20-min swap of notes before November be useful? No agenda, just peers."
Before · bulk-ticket buyer (via Ivan)
"Hi [name] — glad your team's joining KCD Porto. Since a few of you are coming, thought I'd reach out from the xgeeks engineering side — we help teams like yours scale delivery. Worth a quick exploratory call?"
During · at our booth / theirs
"What are you folks building these days? … We run a lot of that nearshore — how's the platform side holding up as you scale?" Curiosity first; let the need surface.
During · at the dinner (manager)
"Loved your team's talk. We pick up exactly this kind of work — happy to connect you with one of our platform leads if it's ever useful, totally exploratory."
Never
Imply sponsoring buys business (or vice-versa); sell from the stage; pitch a competitor. Off-stage, peer-to-peer, neutral — always.
